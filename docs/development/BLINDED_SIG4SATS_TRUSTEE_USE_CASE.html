Bitcoin Trust Administration – In-Development Concept Note

Audience: Professional Bitcoin trust & fiduciary service providers (e.g., Bespoke Group, Onramp Bitcoin).

Educational material – in development now. Forward-looking architecture overview, not legal, tax, or investment advice.

1. Executive Overview

This note outlines how Satnam’s Blinded Authentication × Sig4Sats stack can support Bitcoin education trusts and similar structures.

Core objectives:

  • Encode trust mandates as machine-verifiable contracts, not just PDFs and email threads.
  • Require bonded N-of-M approvals from trustees for high-impact actions.
  • Deliver funds via privacy-preserving Lightning / Cashu rails instead of surveilled banking rails.
  • Maintain a tamper-evident but minimal audit trail for heirs, co-trustees, and auditors.
  • Enable safe automation via a constrained AI “trust officer” agent.

2. Education Trust Use Case at a Glance

Scenario: A family education trust funds a beneficiary’s studies over many years. Distributions are tied to milestones such as enrollment, academic progress, and graduation.

Actors:

  • Trust / trust company (modeled as a “family federation”).
  • Trustees / investment committee (guardian/steward roles).
  • Beneficiary (offspring/adult role).
  • Optional AI “trust officer” agent operating under capped permissions.

3. How the Architecture Works (Phases 0–4)

3.1 Phase 0 – Setup

  • Map trust, trustees, and beneficiary into Satnam’s privacy-first identity model with role-based permissions.
  • Associate a dedicated LNbits wallet and/or Cashu balances with the trust for distributions.

Result: The legal trust structure has a clean, role-based representation without exposing real-world names in application-level logs.

3.2 Phase 1 – Policy & Escrow

  • Trustees co-author an Education Trust Policy event (beneficiary hash, milestones, payout rules, N-of-M approvals).
  • Using attestation credits, the policy and (optionally) hashes of legal documents are timestamped and anchored.
  • Trust assets earmarked for education are converted to Cashu ecash and bound via Sig4Sats to the policy event, creating a cryptographically committed reward pool.

Benefit: Future disputes are resolved by inspecting anchored policy events and escrow commitments, not debating email trails.

3.3 Phase 2 – Milestone Claim & Verification

  • The beneficiary (or a delegated steward) submits a private Milestone Claim event referencing the policy and including encrypted hashes of supporting documents.
  • Trustees review evidence off-platform and, if satisfied, sign a Milestone Verified event.
  • The trust spends attestation credits to anchor that a threshold of trustees verified this milestone.

Benefit: Clear, time-stamped evidence that trustees verified a specific milestone under a specific policy, without exposing beneficiary identities or documents to third parties.

3.4 Phase 3 – Bonded Trustee Approvals

  • For each major payout, trustees receive a Sig4Sats approval offer: stake a small Cashu bond and sign a Milestone Payout Authorization event.
  • Each trustee who agrees stakes their bond and produces a Sig4Sats-backed approval; once N-of-M are collected, the system verifies both signatures and bonded stakes.
  • A blind-issuer function then mints a one-time, blinded payout capability token representing authorization for this specific distribution.

Benefit: Approvals are no longer soft signals; each trustee now has economic skin-in-the-game, with an independent record of who approved what, when, and under which policy.

3.5 Phase 4 – Distribution to Beneficiary

  • The beneficiary’s wallet (or a designated agent) presents the blinded payout token to a payout function.
  • The backend validates the token and executes the payout via Lightning (LNbits) or Cashu release, then marks the token as spent.
  • Optionally, another small attestation records that this milestone was paid out under a specific policy and date.

Benefit: Beneficiaries receive funds via privacy-preserving rails; trustees and auditors retain a precise, cryptographically verifiable record of what was authorized and executed.

4. Extension A – Ongoing Stipends via Blinded Allowance Tokens

After a major milestone payout, trustees may provide ongoing stipends (e.g., monthly living expenses) with strong controls and privacy.

  • Trustees allocate a portion of remaining escrow to a dedicated stipend pool and define a Stipend Policy (cadence, caps, duration).
  • Using Sig4Sats and the stipend policy event, the trust converts Cashu funds into a batch of Blinded Allowance Tokens assigned to the beneficiary.
  • The beneficiary’s wallet holds these tokens; each LN payment or Cashu withdrawal for living expenses consumes one allowance token in addition to normal authentication.
  • The system records only spent-token hashes and aggregate counts, not detailed merchant histories.

Result: Trustees can cap monthly or semester allowances with cryptographic guarantees while beneficiaries retain meaningful financial privacy.

5. Extension B – Trusted AI “Trust Officer” Agent

A Satnam-integrated AI agent can act as a “trust officer” under strict cryptographic constraints rather than unbounded API access.

  • Powers: No master keys or root credentials; limited access to specific attestation credits and bounded sets of allowance and payout tokens, plus read-only access to policy and milestone events.
  • Responsibilities: Monitor upcoming milestones, pre-compute Sig4Sats offers and bond suggestions, execute low-risk, pre-approved stipends within caps, and use its own attestation credits to timestamp key decisions.
  • Governance: If the AI misbehaves or is compromised, damage is capped by the tokens it can access; trustees can revoke or rotate its capabilities by rotating token sets.

6. Why This Matters for Bitcoin Trust Providers

  • Stronger internal controls: Bonded N-of-M approvals and programmable policies, enforced at the cryptographic level.
  • Better records: Cryptographic attestations instead of ambiguous email trails, with minimal data exposure.
  • Improved client privacy: Beneficiaries avoid surveillance-heavy banking rails while still receiving verifiable distributions.
  • Safe automation: AI agents operate within hard, revocable limits defined by blinded tokens and attestation budgets.

This in-development architecture is designed to support existing fiduciary obligations—not bypass them—while giving clients and their families a modern, privacy-respecting trust experience built on Bitcoin, Lightning, and ecash primitives.
